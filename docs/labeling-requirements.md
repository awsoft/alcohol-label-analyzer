### Key Points
- Getting an alcohol label approved by the TTB involves meeting specific requirements that vary by beverage type (distilled spirits, wine, malt beverages).
- Common requirements include brand name, alcohol content, name and address, net contents, and a health warning statement.
- Mandatory information does not all have to appear on one label: placement rules differ by item — the health warning may be on the front, back, or side label, and spirits/malt beverages group brand name, class/type, and alcohol content in one field of vision.
- Additional requirements depend on the type of alcohol, such as disclosures for coloring materials in spirits or sulfite declarations for wine.
- The process can be complex, and it's best to consult TTB resources for full details.

### Overview
The TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau) regulates alcohol labeling in the U.S. to ensure labels are accurate and not misleading. Approval requires submitting a Certificate of Label Approval (COLA) and meeting mandatory labeling requirements, which differ for distilled spirits, wine, and malt beverages. Below, we break down the key steps and requirements for each type.

#### Common Requirements
All alcohol beverages must include:
- **Brand Name**: A prominent, non-misleading name.
- **Alcohol Content**: Stated as a percentage by volume, with specific tolerances.
- **Name and Address**: Of the bottler, packer, or importer, matching the permit.
- **Net Contents**: Volume in metric units (e.g., 750 mL).
- **Health Warning Statement**: A government warning about pregnancy, driving, and health risks, with specific type sizes.
- **Country of Origin**: Required for imported products, per U.S. Customs rules.

#### Type-Specific Requirements
- **Distilled Spirits**: Include class/type designation, disclosures for coloring (e.g., FD&C Yellow #5), wood treatment, sulfites, saccharin, commodity statements, age statements for certain spirits, and state of distillation for U.S. whisky.
- **Wine**: Include class/type, percentage of foreign wine if mentioned, FD&C Yellow No. 5 and cochineal/carmine disclosures, and sulfite declarations if applicable.
- **Malt Beverages (Beer)**: Include class/type, with fewer additional disclosures compared to spirits and wine.

For detailed guidance, check the TTB's labeling resources at [Labeling Resources](https://www.ttb.gov/labeling/labeling-resources).

### Survey Note: Comprehensive Analysis of TTB Alcohol Label Approval Requirements

This note provides an in-depth examination of the requirements for obtaining alcohol label approval from the TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau), as of June 29, 2025. The TTB regulates the labeling of alcohol beverages to ensure consumer protection, prevent deception, and provide adequate product information, as mandated by the Federal Alcohol Administration (FAA) Act. The requirements vary by beverage type—distilled spirits, wine (including cider and mead), and malt beverages (beer)—and are detailed in the TTB's Beverage Alcohol Manuals (BAM) and related regulations.

#### Regulatory Framework and General Considerations
The TTB's labeling regulations are outlined in 27 CFR parts 4 (wine), 5 (distilled spirits), and 7 (malt beverages), with additional guidance from T.D. TTB-158 and T.D. TTB-176, reflecting ongoing modernization efforts. Labels must be submitted for approval via a Certificate of Label Approval (COLA) application, processed through COLAs Online or paper forms, with processing times available at [Processing Times](https://www.ttb.gov/labeling/labeling-resources#processing-times). All labels must be in English, except for brand names, and for Puerto Rico, Spanish is permissible. Imported products must comply with U.S. Customs regulations, including country of origin statements.

A critical universal requirement is the health warning statement, mandated for all containers with ≥0.5% alcohol by volume, bottled after November 18, 1989. This statement, governed by 27 CFR part 16, reads: "GOVERNMENT WARNING: (1) According to the Surgeon General, women should not drink alcoholic beverages during pregnancy because of the risk of birth defects. (2) Consumption of alcoholic beverages impairs your ability to drive a car or operate machinery, and may cause health problems." Per 27 CFR 16.21, the statement may appear on the front, back, or side label, separate and apart from all other information — it is not required to be on the front label. The words "GOVERNMENT WARNING" must appear in capital letters and in bold type, and the remainder of the statement may not appear in bold type. Type sizes vary by container size: 3 mm for >3L, 2 mm for 237 ml–3L, and 1 mm for ≤237 ml, with specific character limits (40 per inch for 1 mm, 25 for 2 mm, 12 for 3 mm) for legibility.

Labels must not be misleading, and additional non-mandatory information (e.g., product descriptions, UPC codes) is allowed if truthful and specific, per 27 CFR 7.56 for malt beverages. The TTB is currently reviewing labeling guidance, with updates aligned with recent regulatory changes, as noted in [Labeling Guidance Review](https://www.ttb.gov/labeling/labeling-resources).

#### Placement of Mandatory Information
Mandatory information does not all have to appear on a single label, and the placement rules differ by commodity:

- **Distilled spirits and malt beverages (27 CFR 5.63 and 7.63, as modernized by T.D. TTB-176)**: The brand name, class/type designation, and alcohol content must appear together in the same field of vision — a single side of the container, normally the front. The modernization eliminated the older "brand label" concept for these commodities. Net contents, the name and address of the bottler/packer or importer, and the country of origin may appear on any label.
- **Wine (27 CFR 4.32, not modernized)**: The brand name and class/type designation must appear on the brand (front) label. Alcohol content, net contents, and the name and address may appear on any label.
- **Health warning statement (27 CFR 16.21)**: May appear on the front, back, or side label, separate and apart from all other information — its absence from any one label is not itself a violation, as long as it appears somewhere on the container.

#### Detailed Requirements by Beverage Type

##### Distilled Spirits
Distilled spirits, regulated under 27 CFR part 5, have extensive mandatory labeling requirements, detailed in the Beverage Alcohol Manual (BAM), Volume 2, last updated January 12, 2022. The following table summarizes the requirements:

| **Requirement**                  | **Details**                                                                 | **Type Size (mm)** | **Legibility & Placement**                                      |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Brand Name                        | Prominent; no misleading names unless descriptive or "BRAND"                | No specific        | Readily legible, contrasting background, same field of vision as class/type and alcohol content (single side, normally front) |
| Class and Type Designation        | Specific identity (e.g., "VODKA"); see Chapter 4                            | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, same field of vision (single side, normally front), contrasting background |
| Alcohol Content                   | % by volume; proof optional, not more prominent; tolerances apply           | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, same field of vision (single side, normally front), contrasting background |
| Name and Address                  | Domestic: bottler/distiller; imported: importer details; see Chapter 5       | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side, contrasting         |
| Country of Origin                 | Required for imports (e.g., "PRODUCT OF [COUNTRY]")                         | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side, contrasting         |
| Net Contents                      | Metric standard of fill; see Chapter 6                                      | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side, contrasting         |
| Presence of Coloring Materials    | Disclose (e.g., "COLORED WITH CARAMEL"); FD&C Yellow #5 specific            | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side (front for specials) |
| Treatment with Wood               | For whisky/brandy: "COLORED AND FLAVORED WITH WOOD [TYPE]"; exceptions apply| 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front                                |
| FD&C Yellow #5 Disclosure         | "CONTAINS FD&C YELLOW #5" if used                                          | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side                      |
| Saccharin Disclosure              | Warning statement if used                                                   | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side                      |
| Sulfite Declaration               | "CONTAINS SULFITES" if ≥10 ppm                                              | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, conspicuous, front/back/side         |
| Commodity Statement               | E.g., "% NEUTRAL SPIRITS DISTILLED FROM [SOURCE]"; see Chapter 4            | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side                      |
| Statements of Age                 | Required for whisky <4 years, grape brandy <2 years; see Chapter 8          | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, varies by type                       |
| State of Distillation             | For U.S. whisky, state if not in name/address; see Item 4                   | 2 (>200 ml), 1 (≤200 ml) | Parallel to base, legible, front/back/side                      |
| Health Warning Statement          | As per general requirements                                                 | 3 (>3L), 2 (237 ml–3L), 1 (≤237 ml) | Legible, contrasting, front/back/side, specific limits         |

Further details are in [Distilled Spirits Labeling](https://www.ttb.gov/distilled-spirits/labeling).

##### Wine
Wine, including cider and mead, is regulated under 27 CFR part 4, with requirements in BAM, Volume 1, last updated January 6, 2022. The table below lists the mandatory requirements:

| **Requirement**                  | **Details**                                                                 | **Type Size (mm)** | **Legibility & Placement**                                      |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Brand Name                        | Prominent, no misleading; 95% from named vineyard for geographic names      | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, brand label, separate                     |
| Class and Type Designation        | State if not defined (e.g., "CHERRY TABLE WINE")                           | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, brand label, separate                     |
| Alcohol Content                   | Required >14% alc; tolerances: ±1% (>14%), ±1.5% (≤14%)                    | ≥1, ≤3 (≤5L)      | Legible, contrasting, any label                                 |
| Percentage of Foreign Wine        | Required if mentioned (e.g., "30% GRAPE WINE FROM ITALY")                   | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, brand label, separate                     |
| Name and Address                  | "BOTTLED BY" or "IMPORTED BY" with address, matches permit                 | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, any label                                 |
| Net Contents                      | Metric, tolerances in 27 CFR 4.37(d)                                       | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, front/back/side                           |
| FD&C Yellow No. 5 Disclosure      | "CONTAINS FD&C YELLOW NO. 5" if used after Oct 6, 1984                     | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, brand/back label                          |
| Cochineal Extract/Carmine         | "CONTAINS COCHINEAL EXTRACT" or "CARMINE" if used after Apr 16, 2013       | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, brand/back label                          |
| Sulfite Declaration               | "CONTAINS SULFITES" if ≥10 ppm; optional <10 ppm statement                 | ≥2 (>187 ml), ≥1 (≤187 ml) | Legible, contrasting, front/back/strip/neck                     |
| Health Warning Statement          | As per general requirements                                                 | 1, 2, 3 (by size)  | Legible, contrasting, front/back/side, specific limits          |
| Country of Origin                 | Required for imports, per 19 CFR 134.11                                    | Not specified      | Not specified, see regulations                                  |

See [Wine Labeling](https://www.ttb.gov/wine/labeling) for more information.

##### Malt Beverages (Beer)
Malt beverages, regulated under 27 CFR part 7, have requirements detailed in BAM, Volume 3, last updated January 29, 2023. The following table summarizes the mandatory requirements:

| **Requirement**                  | **Details**                                                                 | **Type Size (mm)** | **Legibility & Placement**                                      |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Brand Name                        | Must be stated, no specific misleading rules mentioned                      | No specific        | Legible, same field of vision as class/type and alcohol content (single side, normally front) |
| Name and Address                  | Bottler/packer/importer, must match permit                                 | No specific        | Legible, any label                                              |
| Class/Type                        | State type (e.g., "ALE", "LAGER")                                           | No specific        | Legible, same field of vision (single side, normally front)     |
| Net Contents                      | Metric (e.g., "355 mL"), can be blown/embossed                             | No specific        | Legible, front/back/side                                        |
| Alcohol Content                   | % by volume, required                                                      | No specific        | Legible, same field of vision (single side, normally front)     |
| Health Warning Statement          | As per general requirements                                                 | 1, 2, 3 (by size)  | Legible, contrasting, front/back/side, specific limits          |
| Country of Origin                 | Required for imports, per U.S. Customs regulations                          | Not specified      | Not specified, see regulations                                  |

Further details are available at [Malt Beverages Labeling](https://www.ttb.gov/regulated-commodities/beverage-alcohol/beer/labeling).

#### Additional Considerations
- **Allergen Labeling**: Current regulations do not require major food allergen disclosure, but a proposed rulemaking (Notice No. 62, 71 FR 42329) may change this, defining allergens per the Food Allergen Labeling and Consumer Protection Act of 2004 (milk, egg, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, wheat, peanuts, soybeans, and protein-derived ingredients).
- **Organic Claims**: Products labeled "organic" must comply with USDA standards, as noted in [Alcohol Beverage Labeling and Advertising](https://www.ttb.gov/business-central/consumer/alcohol-beverage-labeling-and-advertising).
- **Personalized Labels**: TTB allows personalized labels (e.g., for events) with a template submitted during COLA approval, as per recent final rules, detailed at [TTB Alcohol Label Requirements](https://www.bluelabelpackaging.com/blog/ttb-final-rule-breakdown-the-new-ttb-label-requirements-for-alcohol-labels/).
- **Allowable Changes**: Certain changes (e.g., adding an Internet address) can be made to approved labels without a new COLA, listed in TTB F 5100.31, available at [Allowable Changes](https://www.ttb.gov/labeling/labeling-resources#allowable-changes).
